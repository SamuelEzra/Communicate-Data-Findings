Factors That Influence Child Mortality Rate¶

by Ezra Samuel¶

Investigation Overview¶

The rate of child mortality is a lot higher in some regions than others. For this investigation my intention was to assess factors that influences child mortality rate. The main focus was on: life expectancy, adolescent fertility, sanitation, immunization, income class, and region.

Dataset Overview¶

Five pieces of data were gathered online and merged to form a final data. After some wranging, the final data consisted of 2987 rows. The data was originally gathered from 167 countries between 2000 and 2017. The attributes included the parameters named above as well as others like country name, year, and employment rate. During the exploration phase, one data point was removed due to an apparent error.

Child Mortality Rate Distribution¶

Child mortality rate ranges from about 1 to a bit less than 300 for every 1000 live births. This is a very wide range between the lower and upper limits. Plotting this values on a logarithmic scale produces a multimodal shape of child mortality rate.

A Comparison of Child Mortality with Categorical Variables¶

A box plot of child mortality rate and the categorical variables reveals an interesting trend. It shows that high mortality rate is associated with low income group and the South Asia and Sub-Saharan African regions. Adolescent fertility rate has a similar trend. Life expectancy, sanitation, and immunization are in the reverse order.

Conclusion¶

What can we conclude from the analysis and plots? Certainly, life expectancy and adolescent fertility rate have strong relationships with child mortality rate. For life expectancy, it is a negative relationship while adolescent fertility rate has a negative relationship.

High child mortality rate is also associated with low income economies, and low rate with high income economies. Certain regions like North America and Europe & Central Asia has lower child mortality rates than the Sub-Saharan Africa Region.
